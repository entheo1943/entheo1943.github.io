LSD – Beginner's Guide

Last updated on 2025-02-08.

Introduction

The biggest misconception about LSD is that you hallucinate actual things: dinosaurs in cars, cherry trees in the living room. You don't. LSD is all about perception and perspective. You don't necessarily see large things that are not there. You see what's always been there in a multitude of new ways. Patterns emerge in everything. Your mind emphasizes what it used to ignore. Reality becomes amplified to a staggering degree.

LSD is a very powerful substance which must be respected. This guide has the essential information on the safe usage of LSD. Reading and following this guide will minimize your chances of experiencing a psychedelic crysis (coloquially known as a bad trip).

Serious warnings

  • Those with a personal or family history of mental disorders, particularly psychotic disorders like schizophrenia, should not use LSD without consulting a qualified medical professional. It can precipitate the early onset of schizophrenia in vulnerable individuals.
  • Do not mix LSD with the following drugs/medication: lithium, tricyclic antidepressants, tramadol, ritonavir, deliriants (e.g. DPH) and stimulants (e.g. amphetamine, cocaine). In combination with LSD these drugs can cause bad trips, psychosis, seizures, delusions, anxiety, paranoia, panic attacks and serotonin syndrome.
  • Do not cook, swim, drive or operate heavy machinery while under the influence.
  • Always test your LSD with a reagent test kit or at least get it from a trusted source. LSD tests are cheap and legal to buy. Fake LSD mimics (DOx, 25x-NBOMe) have significantly worse safety profiles than LSD, including the risk of physical overdose and death.
    • If no test kit is available, it is advised to immediately spit out any blotters that have a strong bitter, metallic or chemical-like taste that exceeds a reasonable level.

Dosage and duration

LSD is incredibly potent and dosages are measured in micrograms (µg).

    Dosage:
  • Threshold: 15 µg
  • Light: 15 – 75 µg
  • Common: 75 – 150 µg
  • Strong: 150 – 300 µg
  • Heavy: >300 µg
    Duration:
  • Total: 8 – 12 hours
  • Onset: 15 – 30 minutes
  • Come up: 45 – 90 minutes
  • Peak: 3 – 5 hours
  • Offset: 3 – 5 hours
  • Afterglow: 12 – 48 hours

Drug combinations

  • Cannabis has a strong and volatile synergy with LSD. It's often used to intensify or prolong LSD's effects. Combine with cannabis with caution as this can cause a bad trip, anxiety, paranoia, panic attacks and psychosis.
  • Antidepressants, antipsychotics and benzodiazepines reduce the LSD's effects. Benzodiazepines (e.g. Xanax, Valium) are often used to mellow down the trip in the case of overwhelming anxiety or psychosis.
  • Alcohol – alcohol's depressant effects can be used to reduce some of the anxiety and muscle tension produced by LSD. However, alcohol can also cause dehydration, nausea, and physical fatigue, which can negatively impact the user's mindset (set).
  • MDMA – LSD synergistically enhances the physical, cognitive, and visual effects of MDMA. The synergy between these substances is unpredictable and so it is advised to start with markedly lower doses than one would take for either individually. There is some evidence that suggests LSD can increase the neurotoxicity of MDMA.

Tolerance

LSD is not addictive. There are no withdrawal symptoms when chronic use is stopped. LSD is not in any way harmful physically to the body. LSD has no known toxic dose; it is effectively impossible to physically overdose on.

Common routine drug tests cannot detect LSD in the system. Generally speaking, LSD will remain in your blood and saliva for up to 24 hours, in your urine for 1 to 3 days, and in your hair for up to 90 days.

Full tolerance to LSD is formed in as little as 3 hours after ingestion. LSD also exhibits cross-tolerance with all other psychedelics, meaning that after the use of LSD, all psychedelics will have a reduced effect.

It's recommended to wait 10–14 days between trips for the tolerance to fully reset. You may use this calculator for a rough estimate.

How to ingest

LSD is usually distributed in the form of blotters (aka tabs) – small paper squares dipped in a solution of LSD and alcohol. In this form, LSD is ingested orally, which means you can simply swallow the tabs

However, people often hold the tabs under the tongue (sublingually) and lightly chew and suck on them for 15 minutes or until they become soggy, and then swallow them. Taking LSD sublingually will reduce the waiting time for the effects, as LSD is absorbed directly into the bloodstream through the lingual artery.

Don't drink water 30 minutes before and 30 minutes after ingesting LSD. The chlorine in the water can completely destroy LSD.

Tips for a good trip

  • A first time dose should be around 100 µg (1 blotter).
  • Have some benzodiazepine on hand (e.g. Xanax – alprazolam, Valium – diazepam) to calm down from a bad trip in the case of overwhelming anxiety or psychosis. If you have no tolerance to benzodiazepines, a single medicinal dose should be enough.
    Benzodiazepines will not completely abort a trip, but it will remove anxiety and the psychedelic thoughts. It will also suppress the stimulating effects of LSD.
  • If inexperienced or when experimenting with large dosages, you may have one trusted individual as a trip sitter – a person who stays sober to ensure safety of the drug user.
  • The effects of LSD last for 8-12 hours, depending on the person and the dosage. It's impossible to sleep while under the influence. Plan your day with that in mind. It's best to start the trip in the late morning or early afternoon.
  • Have no responsibilities or chores nagging you on the day of the trip.
  • If you are inexperienced, you should have your trip in a safe, comfortable and familiar environment.
  • Remove any dangerous objects (e.g. guns, knives) from the room.
  • Eat a small and healthy meal 1-2 hours before dosing, because you completely lose apetite under the influence.
  • Don't trip if you feel physically unwell or sick. Don't trip if you're depressed or in a bad mood.
  • Use chewing gum to alleviate jaw clenching.
  • Have sunglasses ready if you want to hide your dilated pupils from other people in public.
  • Music is essential for a good LSD trip. Prepare a long playlist and have decent headphones on hand.
