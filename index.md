This is a short usage manual for using LSD. It covers the bare essentials you must know before ingesting this substance. By informing yourself about LSD, you increase your chances of having a positive experience.
LSD is a powerful drug which must be treated with respect.

### Risks & warnings

Those with a personal or family history of mental disorders, particularly psychotic disorders like schizophrenia, should not use LSD without consulting a qualified medical professional.

Do not mix LSD with the following drugs/medication: lithium, tricyclic antidepressants, tramadol, ritonavir, deliriants (e.g. DPH) and stimulants (e.g. amphetamine, cocaine).

SSRI’s, benzodiazepines and alcohol reduce the LSD effects. Benzodiazepines are often used as *trip killers* to abort the trip in the case of overwhelming anxiety or psychosis

Cannabis has a strong, volatile synergy with LSD. It’s often used to intensify or prolong LSD's effects. Combine with cannabis with caution as this can cause a bad trip, anxiety, paranoia, panic attacks, and psychosis.

Do not swim, drive or operate heavy machinery while under the influence of LSD.

Always test your LSD with a reagent test kit or at least get it from a trusted source. Fake LSD mimics (DOx, 25x-NBOMe) have significantly worse safety profiles than LSD, including the risk of physical overdose and death.

Unlike unadulterated LSD blotters, which are usually only mildly bitter or slightly metallic as a byproduct of the blotter paper's ink, mimics are described as having a "metallic", "numbing", "chemical-like", or "extremely bitter or sour" taste. It is advised to immediately spit out any blotters that have a strong bitter taste that exceeds a reasonable level.
### Planning

The effects of LSD last for 8-12 hours, depending on the person and the dosage. It’s impossible to sleep while under the influence. Plan your day with that in mind. It’s best to start the trip in the late morning or early afternoon.

Have no responsibilities or chores nagging you on the day of the trip.

Eat a small and healthy meal 1-2 hours before dosing, because you completely lose apetite under the influence.

Don’t trip if you feel physically unwell or sick. Don’t trip if you’re depressed or in a bad mood.

Use chewing gum to alleviate jaw clenching.

Have sunglasses ready if you want to hide your dilated pupils from other people.

Music is essential for a good LSD trip. Prepare a long playlist and have decent headphones on hand.
### How to ingest

Most commonly, LSD is ingested orally, which means you can simply swallow the tabs. 

However, people often hold the tabs under the tongue (sublingually) and lightly chew and suck on them for 15 minutes or until they become soggy, and then swallow them. Taking LSD sublingually will reduce the waiting time for the effects, as LSD is absorbed directly into the bloodstream through the lingual artery.

Don’t drink water 30 minutes before and 30 minutes after ingesting LSD. The chlorine in the water can completely destroy LSD.

### Tolerance

LSD is not addictive. There are no withdrawal symptoms when chronic use is stopped. LSD is not in any way harmful physically to the body. LSD has no known toxic dose; it is effectively impossible to physically overdose on.

Regular drug tests cannot detect LSD in the system.

Full tolerance to LSD is formed in as little as 3 hours after ingestion. LSD also exhibits cross-tolerance with all other psychedelics, meaning that after the use of LSD, all psychedelics will have a reduced effect.

It’s recommended to wait 10–14 days between trips for the tolerance to fully reset.

### Mindset

To have a good trip, always remember to completely surrender to the experience. Let the LSD take you on a journey. Don’t resist anything. Don’t take things too seriously. Remember that the hallucinations aren’t real.
Write a physical reminder that you took LSD, and how much you took. Remember that you’re under the influence of a drug – you will eventually return to your normal self.
